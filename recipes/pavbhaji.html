PAO BHAJI




DESCRIPTION

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish that originated in Mumbai and is now loved all over India — and even abroad. It consists of two main parts: Bhaji – a thick, spicy vegetable mash made by cooking potatoes, tomatoes, peas, capsicum (bell peppers), onions, and sometimes cauliflower together with butter and a special pav bhaji masala (a blend of aromatic spices). The mixture is mashed into a smooth consistency and simmered to absorb all the flavors. Pav – soft bread rolls, typically lightly toasted with butter on a hot griddle until golden.

Pav Bhaji is famous as a quick, filling, and affordable meal, often eaten as a snack or dinner, and is especially loved at roadside stalls where the aroma of butter and spices fills the air.

DIRECTIONS

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and 1/4 teaspoon salt; fry until browned, 5 to 8 minutes. Add garlic; cook and stir for 4 minutes. Pour in 1 cup water. Add 1 teaspoon pav bhaji masala and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until flavors combine, about 10 minutes.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and 1/4 teaspoon salt; fry until browned, 5 to 8 minutes. Add garlic; cook and stir for 4 minutes. Pour in 1 cup water. Add 1 teaspoon pav bhaji masala and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until flavors combine, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add 1/4 to 1/2 cup water to give curry a runny consistency. Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil and butter; stir until butter is melted. Divide into serving bowls and sprinkle remaining pav bhaji masala on top.
